Normalization Exercise

Improve the provided Music design by applying tests and fixes for the first three normal forms.

Goal

  • practice identifying and naming normal form violations
  • use design to fix normal form violations
  • represent a physical design in an ERD

Requirements

Submit: two files

  • a text file report of your analysis
  • a graphical ERD

Your improved design should fix at least 5 normal form violations. (You should have at least 5 attributes that are now in a better entity or have been changed so they better fit the relational model.)

Your report should include:

  • the answer to “what is a natural key for Track?
  • answers to questions about Playlist
  • for each change you have made:
    • what entity/attribute(s) is a problem?
    • what normal form are you fixing

Your design must:

  • be in ERD format
  • show all link tables and PK/FK attributes used in relations (physical design)
  • accurately show relation lines

Design to fix

Analysis questions

Please answer these questions before starting on your changes:

  • what attribute(s) form a natural key for Track? (A composite key is OK.)
  • playlist questions:
    • what do you want the maximum number of songs/tracks in a playlist to be?
    • do you want to let the user define an order to the playlist?
    • do you want the user to put the same track in one playlist more than once?

The above questions are required.

Optional Analysis Questions

Some changes you make to the Music database design may raise questions about what the customer (you, ideally!) wants. Feel free to think through to the best answer you can, record your thoughts, and then continue with your design.

Some questions about the Music database might be:

  • how do you handle Singles?
  • how should genre work for a playlist?
  • are there other contributors to a work than writers, vocalists, and instrumentalists?
    • how might someone use this information?
  • what does “year” really mean?
  • does it handle classical music well?

Suggestions

  • check the meaning of your primary keys. PKs must be unique and non-null.
  • take care to ensure accuracy of PK/FK relationship lines
  • remember that Normalization is a design check: you should fix broken features, but it is not the time to add new features

Note: the diagram points rely on ERD format; if your design is not in ERD format, 20 points are at risk. Please use the ERD format from class and examples.

Note: remember to say in the text file which entities you have moved and what normal form violation caused you to move them. This makes the normal form framework work for you (by confirming your identification and suggesting a good fix), and it helps me see, understand, and give better credit for your change. I suggest you write this text before you change the diagram.

Rubric

This exercise is worth 90 points.

Prologue questions, to be answered in text document (20 points total, from following:)

  • 8 points: what is a natural key for Track?
  • 4 points: what do you want the maximum items in a playlist to be?
  • 4 points: do you want to let the user define an order to the playlist?
  • 4 points: do you want the user to put the same track in one playlist more than once?

Improvements, which should be recorded in the text document and shown in the Entity-Relation Diagram. Each improvement is scored by the following metric. (These sum to 14 points available per change. Five perfect improvements yields 70 points. You may make more than five fixes.)

  • 4 points: identify violation (describe in text file)
  • 4 points: move attributes to the correct table
  • 3 points: key or many-to-many correct in entity
  • 2 points: foreign key correctly identified in entity
  • 1 points: ERD lines match FK/PK
